Getting There

Jotunheimen National Park is located in the centre of Norway and is quite easily accessible from Oslo by bus. There is a seasonal bus service (line NW161) running from Oslo straight to Gjendesheim where this hike begins. The bus journey takes around 4 hours. More details including prices and timetables can be found on the bus operator's website , where you can also book tickets.

Description

Jotunheimen is the most mountainous region in Norway and contains Norway's highest peak. This hike takes you through some of the most stunning mountain scenery found in Europe. The going can sometimes be quite difficult due to the steep mountain slopes and very rocky terrain, especially at higher altitudes. Finding a spot to pitch up your tent can sometimes be challenging so it is always better to plan to camp a lower altitudes were there are more clear spaces without large boulders. Water is abundant throughout the national park. As the whole region is above the tree line there is little chance of gathering enough wood to make a fire, so a good gas stove and enough gas is essential.

Getting There

Northern Finland north of Rovaniemi generally has very little transport links. It is possible to catch a bus from Rovaniemi to the western edge of the national park, but this is still quite far from the eastern region where the highest peaks are. The starting point of this hike is thus only reachable by car and involves driving around 1-2h off the main road on a dirt track. There are many car rental options available in Rovaniemi.

Description

Urhro Kekkonen national park is a large area in north Finland bordering Russia. It is one of very few mountainous areas in Finland, but the heights are still a lot lower than in Sweden or Norway. Most valleys are still under the tree line. The whole region is basically a huge pine forest which may make navigation a bit tricky sometimes as the trees can cover out the features of the landscape. On the up side there is always enough wood to make a fire if the night gets chilly. The above features combined means that this national park is great for easy hikes with little elevation.

About Us

We are a couple of hiking enthusiasts based in the UK who migrate north every summer to explore some of the last untouched wildernesses in Europe. We hope the information on this page will help others who wish to do the same. All of our hikes involve wild camping, which is fully legal in Scandinavia as long as you respect your surroundings.
